#  Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## 📄 Overview
This data analytics project provides an end-to-end analysis of customer shopping habits using a transactional dataset of 3,900 purchases across various product categories. The objective is to identify key spending patterns, demographic trends, and product performance metrics to guide strategic business decisions. By cleaning and preparing the data in Python, running structured relational database queries in SQL Server Management Studio (SSMS), and building an interactive dashboard in Power BI, this project delivers actionable, data-driven strategies to optimize advertising spend, loyalty programs, and subscription growth.

---

##  📊 Dataset Summary
* **Data Volume:** 3,900 customer transaction records
* **Attributes:** 18 distinct features
* **Core Metrics:**
  * **Demographics:** Customer ID, Age, Gender, Location, Subscription Status
  * **Purchase Data:** Item Purchased, Category, Purchase Amount (USD), Season, Size, Color
  * **Behavioral Indicators:** Discount Applied, Promo Code Used, Previous Purchases, Frequency of Purchases, Review Rating, Shipping Type
* **Data Quality:** Handled 37 missing values originally present in the `Review Rating` column.

##  ⚙️ Project Steps

###  1. Data Cleaning & EDA (Python)
* Ingested the raw dataset and evaluated baseline distributions using `df.info()` and `describe()`.
* Handled 37 missing values in the `Review Rating` column by applying category-specific median imputation.
* Renamed all columns to standard `snake_case` for clean programming architecture and optimal readability.
* Engineered custom attributes to segment data: `age_group` (demographic binning) and `purchase_frequency_days`.
* Dropped the redundant `promo_code_used` column after checking data consistency against `discount_applied`.
* Connected the Python script to a local SQL Server instance using `pyodbc` and pushed the cleaned data into the database for detailed analysis.

###  2. Deep-Dive Business Analysis (SQL)
Executed relational database queries within SSMS to extract targeted business performance markers:
* **Revenue by Gender:** Compared total revenue generated by male ($157,890) versus female ($75,191) customers.
* **High-Spending Discount Users:** Identified **839 unique customers** who utilized discount offers but still spent above the average purchase amount per transaction.
* **Top 5 Products by Rating:** Isolated items with the highest customer review scores: Gloves (3.86), Sandals (3.84), Boots (3.82), Hat (3.80), and Skirt (3.78).
* **Fulfillment Comparison:** Evaluated average transaction spend between Standard shipping ($58.46) and Express shipping ($60.48) tiers.
* **Subscription Breakdown:** Profiled spending metrics across membership classes:
  * *Subscribers (Yes):* 1,053 customers | $59.49 average spend | $62,645.00 total revenue.
  * *Non-Subscribers (No):* 2,847 customers | $59.87 average spend | $170,436.00 total revenue.
* **Discount-Dependent Products:** Discovered the top 5 items most reliant on promotions based on their percentage of discounted sales: Hat (50.00%), Sneakers (49.66%), Coat (49.07%), Sweater (48.17%), and Pants (47.37%).
* **Customer Lifecycle Segmentation:** Classified the audience based on total historical purchase frequency into Loyal (3,116 customers), Returning (701 customers), and New (83 customers) segments.
* **Category Best-Sellers:** Ranked the top 3 most-purchased products within each major category (e.g., Jewelry, Blouses, Sandals, and Jackets taking top spots).
* **Retention vs. Membership:** Checked whether frequent buyers (with >5 purchases) are actively subscribing, identifying a massive group of 2,518 unsubscribed repeat buyers versus 958 subscribed repeat buyers.
* **Demographic Revenue Generation:** Tracked absolute financial contributions by age segment: Young Adult ($62,143), Middle-aged ($59,197), Adult ($55,978), and Senior ($55,763).

###  3. Visual Intelligence (Power BI)
* Standardized relational data models and built interactive database connections.
* Designed an executive-ready dashboard showcasing top performance KPIs, categorical spend distributions, and audience mix parameters.

##  📈 Key Results & Business Insights

* **Primary Revenue Driver:** Male shoppers contribute more than double the absolute revenue ($157.8K) of female shoppers ($75.1K), marking them as a critical marketing demographic.
* **Premium Shipping Advantage:** Shoppers opting for Express Shipping demonstrate higher average transaction sizes ($60.48), pointing to opportunities for fast-shipping incentives.
* **Conversion Runway:** A significant cohort of **2,518 repeat buyers** already love the brand (>5 historical purchases) but remain unsubscribed, presenting an ideal target for conversion campaigns.
* **Discount Vulnerability:** Products like Hats and Sneakers are bought with discounts roughly 50% of the time, revealing a high reliance on markdowns that compromises product profit margins.
